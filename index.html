How Our System Works: A Step-by-Step Guide

Making sense of e-waste classification and material recovery

Step 1: Identify What You Have

Our AI system recognizes different types of electronic devices

When you input an item, our smart computer vision system analyzes:

  • What it looks like: Is it a laptop? Phone? Circuit board? Power supply?
  • Its condition: Is it working, damaged, or hazardous?
  • What's inside: Does it contain valuable metals like gold and copper?

Accuracy: 95% - We get it right 19 out of 20 times

📋 4-Tier Classification System

Every item gets sorted into one of these categories:

Class What It Means Examples What Happens Next
A Repair & Resale Grade
Like-new condition		 Working laptops, phones, monitors Cleaned & sold for reuse
B High-Value Recovery
Contains precious metals		 Circuit boards, gold fingers, connectors Sent for metal extraction
C Standard Recycling
Mixed materials		 Plastic housings, mixed metals, glass Material separation & recovery
D Hazardous Waste
Needs special handling		 Batteries, swollen components, contaminated items Safe disposal by certified facility

Step 2: Extract the Valuable Materials

Here's how we get the gold, copper, and other precious metals out

The Bioleaching Process

We use a natural, eco-friendly method where special bacteria help dissolve and separate metals from electronic waste.

Recovery Rate: 99.5% - We extract almost every precious metal (not just scratching the surface!)

Why This Matters

  • Environmental: Reduces mining for new metals, saves natural resources
  • Economic: Recovers valuable materials worth thousands per tonne
  • Operational: Enables safe, organized e-waste processing
  • Compliance: Meets all environmental and safety regulations

How We Identify Items (Technical Details)

Our AI system uses multiple methods to be super accurate:

Visual Recognition: Identifies circuit board types and components using deep learning

Material Analysis: Detects precious metals based on appearance and patterns

Cross-Reference: Compares against database of 50,000+ known devices

The result? 95% accuracy - better than manual sorting and incredibly fast!
